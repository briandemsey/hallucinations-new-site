Glossary of Terms

Key concepts in AI verification and hallucination detection.

AI Hallucination
A phenomenon where an AI model generates information that is false, misleading, or fabricated, presented with the same confidence as accurate information. Hallucinations can range from minor factual errors to completely invented citations, events, or entities.
H-Score
A proprietary reliability metric developed by Hallucinations.cloud that rates AI outputs across four dimensions: Safety, Trust, Confidence, and Quality. Scores range from 0-100, with higher scores indicating greater reliability.
Multi-Model Verification
The practice of submitting the same query to multiple AI models and comparing responses to identify inconsistencies, contradictions, and potential hallucinations. Consensus across models increases confidence in accuracy.
Truth Verification Engine
A system that cross-references AI-generated claims against trusted sources, including academic databases, official records, and authoritative publications to validate factual accuracy.
Red Team / Blue Team / Purple Team
A methodology borrowed from cybersecurity. Red team attempts to find flaws in AI outputs, blue team defends accuracy and reliability, and purple team synthesizes findings to improve overall verification.
Confidence Score
A component of the H-Score that measures certainty level based on model agreement, source availability, and the specificity of the claim being verified.
Model Consensus
When multiple AI models provide substantially similar answers to the same query. High consensus generally indicates higher reliability, though all models can share common training data biases.
